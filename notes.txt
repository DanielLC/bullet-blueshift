I'm trying to make the exploding star from the original.

I accelerate for a certain amount of time, then fire a bullet which slows to a stop, while moving twice that distance before firing another bullet, and repeat. I also need to make sure times are synchronized, so I'll need both t and tau (or as I've been calling it, s). And I'll say x is distance. And n is the number of bullets.

Given v, total_distance, n
x = total_distance/(2*n)
rapidity = atanh(v)
rapidity = a*tau
t = sinh(tau*a) / a
x^2 = t^2 + 1 / a^2

Let's try this with a and x instead.
t = sqrt(x^2 - a^-2)
tau = asinh(t*a)
v = tanh(a*tau)
gamma = 1/sqrt(1-v^2)
	= cosh(a*tau)

First, accelerate at a for tau, then fire a bullet accelerating at -a for tau
Next, it has to move a distance of 2x. Which without relativity, would be a time of 2x/v, but with it, it's 2x/(gamma*v) = 2x/sinh(a*tau).
